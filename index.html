What is a conversion — and why does it matter?

A conversion is the moment a visitor does what you want them to do. It's the action that turns a stranger on Google into a real lead for your business. Without tracking conversions, we'd only know how many people clicked the ad — not how many actually contacted you.

Think of it this way: clicks are visitors walking into your shop. Conversions are the ones who actually ask to speak to someone.

What counts as a conversion for your campaign
Counts as a conversion
Filling in your contact form
Clicking to call from the ad
Clicking a "get a quote" button
Does not count
Just viewing the ad
Clicking and leaving immediately
Visiting without taking action

We configure conversion tracking before we spend a single rand. Every enquiry gets recorded so we always know which keywords and ads are generating real leads — not just traffic.

Your landing page needs work before a single rand is spent

Google Ads drives people to your Compliance Risk Management page. That page is where the money is made or lost. In its current state, sending paid traffic there would be like paying to send customers to a shop with the lights off and the doors locked.

Without these fixes, your cost per lead will be high and the campaign will underperform — and Google Ads will get the blame for a landing page problem. Here is exactly what needs to change, and why.

Critical — blocks all conversions
1. No call-to-action anywhere on the page

There is no form, no button, no way for a visitor to take the next step. Every conversion we track — form submissions, quote requests, consultation bookings — requires at least one of these to exist first.

The fix

Add a short lead capture form above the fold — the part of the page visible without scrolling. Do not use a generic "Contact Us" link. For compliance clients, a high-value offer converts far better: "Request a Free Compliance Health Check" or "Book a 30-Minute FSCA Consultation." FSPs respond to specific, low-risk first steps.

Critical — causes immediate bounce
2. The page doesn't match what people searched for

If someone searches "FICA compliance training" and lands on a page about "Risk Management Frameworks," they leave within seconds. The words on the page must mirror the words in the ad. This is called message match, and it is the single biggest driver of bounce rate.

The fix

Add a clear "Our Specialised Services" section with three distinct columns — one each for FAIS Compliance, FICA Training, and Outsourced Compliance Officers. Each column needs 2–3 bullet points of what is actually delivered: monthly audits, FSCA reporting, staff training manuals.

If a full page rewrite is not possible before launch, this section alone — added to the existing page — will close most of the gap.

Important — high mobile lead loss
3. The phone number is not click-to-call on mobile

Financial professionals search for "outsourced compliance officer" between meetings, on their phones. If they have to copy and paste a number into their dialer, you lose roughly half of those mobile leads before they happen.

The fix

Wrap the phone number in a tel: link so it opens the dialer automatically on mobile. Make it prominent in the header — not buried in the footer. This is a one-line developer change.

Important — compliance niche requires trust
4. No credibility signals for a trust-driven niche

The compliance space runs on trust. FSPs are not making casual decisions — they are entrusting someone with their regulatory standing. The current page gives them no reason to believe NFS Group has done this before.

The fix

Add the following — any combination works: the FSCA logo or authorisation reference if permitted, the FSP categories served, years of experience, Certified Compliance Practitioner credentials, and one strong client quote about a specific compliance outcome NFS Group delivered.

One genuine client quote — "NFS Group kept us audit-ready through our FSCA renewal with zero disruption to our operations" — does more for conversion than three paragraphs of capability description.

Recommended — keeps tracking in your control
5. Use Google Tag Manager for conversion tracking

Waiting for a developer to hard-code every tracking event creates delays and dependency. If the developer is slow, your campaign runs blind.

The fix

Install Google Tag Manager (GTM) on the site. This allows button clicks, form submissions and page events to be tracked without touching the website code each time. One GTM installation by the developer gives us ongoing control of all conversion tracking independently.

How the campaign is technically built to perform on Google

There is more to a Google Ads campaign than writing headlines and pressing go. Below is how the technical setup protects your budget, improves your position on Google, and makes sure the right people — and only the right people — see your ads.

Keyword match types — controlling who sees your ads

Keywords on their own are not enough — you have to tell Google how loosely or tightly to match them. Without this, your "FAIS compliance services" keyword can trigger ads for "FAIS compliance jobs" or "what is FAIS compliance" — searches with zero purchase intent that cost you money.

Exact match
Ad only shows when the search is very close to your keyword. Highest control, lowest volume. Used for your most valuable terms.
Phrase match
Ad shows when the search contains your keyword phrase in the right order. Balanced control and reach. Used for supporting terms.

No broad match is used in Month 1. Broad match hands Google too much control before there is enough conversion data to guide it.

Ad groups — each keyword cluster gets its own matched ad

Rather than running one ad against all four keywords, the campaign is structured into themed ad groups — FICA, FAIS, Outsourced Officers, and Regulatory Consulting. Each group has headlines written to match that specific search. This improves ad relevance scores and reduces cost per click.

FICA ad group FAIS ad group Outsourced officer ad group Regulatory consulting ad group
Display URL paths — the address shown in the ad

The URL that appears in your Google ad is not the same as the actual link — it is a display address that should match what the person searched for. A generic URL reduces click-through rate. A matched URL reinforces trust.

Not this nfsgroup.co.za
This nfsgroup.co.za/FAIS-Compliance
This nfsgroup.co.za/FICA-Training
This nfsgroup.co.za/Compliance-Officer
Ad extensions — making your ad take up more space on the page

Extensions are additional lines that appear below your ad headline for free. They push competitor ads further down the page and give users more reasons to click. Google also rewards advertisers who use them with better ad positioning.

Sitelinks — direct links to FAIS Compliance, FICA Training, Outsourced Officers, Book a Consultation
Callouts — "FSCA Registered" · "Gauteng-Based Team" · "CPD-Accredited Provider" · "25+ Years Experience"
Structured snippets — Services: FAIS Monitoring, FICA Training, FSCA Applications, Regulatory Audits
Lead form extension — a short enquiry form that appears directly on Google, before the user even visits the website. Critical stopgap while the landing page is being updated.
Call extension — your phone number displayed directly in the ad with one-tap calling on mobile
Bid strategy — Month 1 is data collection, not optimisation

Google's smart bidding (Target CPA, Maximise Conversions) requires at least 30–50 recorded conversions before it can optimise reliably. A brand new account has none. Launching on smart bidding with no data produces erratic, expensive results. Month 1 runs on Maximise Clicks with a manual CPC cap to build the conversion dataset. Smart bidding is introduced in Month 2 once the data supports it.

Mobile bid adjustment — protecting budget until the page is ready

Until the landing page has a click-to-call button and a mobile-optimised form, mobile clicks will produce near-zero conversions. A −30% mobile bid adjustment is applied at launch to reduce spend on mobile traffic. Once the page is mobile-ready, the adjustment is removed and mobile bidding is opened up fully.
